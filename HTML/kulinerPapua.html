TRADITIONAL FOOD


"Exploring the Flavors of Papua: A Journey Through Traditional Cuisine"

Asap Asap Fish


A type of fish that has been
smoked until dry.


Papeda


A type of congee made from sago
starch with a sticky texture and
bland taste.


Udang Selingkuh


Udang selingkuh are freshwater
shrimp with shells as hard as
lobsters, and claws resembling
crab claws



Sago Plate Cake


A dried cake made from sago, usually eaten as a snack or side dish.


Sago Catterpillar


Skewered smoked caterpillars served resembling satay with soft filling.


Yellow Broth Fish


A fish cooked with Papuan yellow spices, served with papeda or rice.


LOCAL RESTAURANT


"Savoring Tradition: A Culinary Journey Through Papua's Local Flavors"

YUGOWA RESTAURANT


Sentani, Jayapura


Yougwa restaurant boasts a stunning view overlooking Lake Sentani, making it ideal for an afternoon visit while awaiting dinner. The menu features authentic Papuan traditional cuisine.


RUMAH LAUT CAFE AND RESTO


Numbai, Jayapura


Enjoy dinner at a restaurant above the sea with a view of the night atmosphere of Jayapura city. Rumah Laut is a Papuan seafood restaurant with quite the popularity in Jayapura in terms of taste and view.


DUTA CAFE LESEHAN ATAS LAUT


Numbai, Jayapura


Experience stunning vistas, live music, savor fresh seafood, and indulge in delightful spices while dining above the sea. Observe the clear sights of the sea, harbor, and Jayapura hills, all while enjoying authentic Papuan cuisine.
