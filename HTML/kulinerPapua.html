Asap Asar Fish


Asar Fish is fish that is smoked until dry. The most commonly used fish are skipjack tuna, yellowtail fish, and mackerel. Asar fish has long been a part of the life of the people of Jayapura. Whether consumed daily or as souvenirs for tourists, this grilled fish is indeed delicious.


Grilled fish is basically the same as smoked fish. The difference lies in the way it is smoked, which is by positioning the fish diagonally on the side of the smoke.


Papeda


Papeda, or sago porridge, is a type of congee made from sago starch. Usually served with tongkol or burbara fish seasoned with turmeric. Papeda is white and has a sticky texture resembling glue with a bland taste. Papeda is rich in fiber, low in cholesterol, and nutritious. Papeda has many health benefits, one of which is to cleanse the lungs for smokers.


Papeda is included in Indonesia's Intangible Cultural Heritage by UNESCO.


Udang Selingkuh


Udang selingkuh is a freshwater shrimp with a shell as hard as a lobster's and claws resembling those of a crab. This uniqueness has earned it the nickname udang selingkuh, which translated into cheating shrimp, as it is considered the result of a cheating affair between a lobster and a crab.


This shrimp has a texture similar to lobster, with firm and fibrous flesh that is very tender and slightly sweet. It is commonly served with oyster sauce, butter sauce, or Padang sauce with water spinach or papaya flowers as accompaniments, making udang selingkuh dish taste even more special.


Sago Plate Cake


This food is a type of dried cake made from sago. Typically, the people of Papua enjoy sagu lempeng as a snack, often paired with a cup of warm tea. Sagu lempeng can also be eaten with various dishes or served with thick gravy, such as curry gravy. Sagu lempeng is quite nutritious, rich in carbohydrates and fiber.


Sagu lempeng is part of Papua's culinary heritage and is often served at cultural events, weddings, or other traditional ceremonies.


Sago Catterpillar


Papua's sago caterpillar is usually served almost like satay. The savory sago caterpillar has thin skin and soft flesh. These caterpillars are smoked first to make them slightly dry and more durable. They are then fried and eaten with rice..


The Kamoro people believe that sago caterpillars are a healthy food due to their high protein and vitamin content.


Yellow Broth Fish


Yellow broth fish is fish cooked with typical yellow spices from Papua, served with papeda or rice. Papeda with yellow broth fish is usually served during important celebrations.


In its presentation, papeda is poured into a serving plate using a type of chopsticks called "gata-gata," which is done by quickly rotating the dough so that the papeda dough does not break when rolled. After adding the yellow broth, sip the papeda at the edge of the plate while sipping the fresh yellow broth.
