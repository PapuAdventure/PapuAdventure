“Where nature thrives unbothered by human”

Papua is a land of unparalleled natural beauty and exotic wildlife, awaiting those with

Wonders

Papua’s scenery are all out of this world, offering a untouched wilderness and vibrant biodiversity that can’t be seen anywhere else. From the mist-shrouded peaks of the Jayawijaya Mountains to the crystal-clear waters of Raja Ampat's coral reefs, Papua's landscapes are a testament to nature's boundless creativity. Along the coast, pristine beaches stretch for miles, fringed by swaying palms and kissed by the gentle lapping of the ocean waves. Whether exploring dense jungles, diving into underwater wonderlands, Papua’s natural wonders will never fail to inspire awe and wonder

Papeda

One of the traditional Papuan dish that holds cultural significance and culinary delight is Papeda, made from sago starch, this thick, glutinous pudding is a staple food in Papua, cherished for its simplicity and versatility. Typically served with fish or other protein- rich accompaniments, Papeda is enjoyed for its unique texture and subtle flavor. Papeda is a comforting and satisfying dish that reflects the deep connection between the Papuan people and their natural surroundings.

Cendrawasih

Burung Cendrawasih, or the bird of paradise is an iconic bird in the heart of Papua. They are known to captivate the imaginations of many with their eye- catching feathers and mesmerizing looks. With their iridescent hues and intricate patterns, this embodiment of beauty is a masterpiece of evolution, adapted to thrive in the dense rain-forests of the region. Visitors to Papua are often drawn to bird-watching expeditions in search of these elusive avian wonders, hoping to catch a glimpse of their ethereal beauty amidst the lush greenery of the jungle. Though these are just one of many alluring wildlife in Papua.

Asmat Tribe

The Asmat tribe, indigenous to the remote regions of Papua, Indonesia, is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and intricate woodcarvings. Living in the dense mangrove swamps along the southern coast, the Asmat people have developed a unique way of life deeply rooted in their natural surroundings. The Asmat's intricate artwork, often crafted from the wood of the mangrove trees that surround their villages, serves as a form of cultural expression and a means of preserving their oral traditions and spiritual beliefs.
